Roofing intake

What roofing problem are you trying to solve?

Tell us the issue, urgency, and ZIP. Production requests will route one provider at a time with explicit consent.

Murfreesboro Home Pros is a research and referral service, not a contractor. Provider routing requires homeowner consent before a request is shared. Providers are responsible for estimates, contracts, permits, work, warranties, and outcomes.

Common roofing projects

Match the roof problem before matching the roofer.

Leak triage

Water stain, ceiling drip, or soft decking concern.

Urgency matters. The intake should separate emergency leak control from full replacement planning.

Storm damage

Wind, hail, missing shingles, or insurance questions.

Homeowners need inspection clarity, photo documentation, and a provider who communicates cleanly.

Replacement planning

Older roof, repeated repairs, or selling the home soon.

Estimate detail, warranty language, ventilation, and material fit become more important than speed alone.

Inspection first

Not sure if it is roof, gutter, flashing, or ventilation.

The right first step may be a roof inspection — not a high-pressure replacement conversation.

Neighborhood signal

Roofing proof should be close to home.

Future provider profiles should connect roofing examples to nearby areas, not generic stock portfolios.

Growth corridors

Blackman, Cason Lane, and west-side growth.

Newer homes can raise different questions around materials, ventilation, builder-era details, and warranty expectations.

Established areas

Downtown, north Murfreesboro, and older roofs.

Age, decking condition, prior repairs, and estimate clarity matter when repair history is unknown.
